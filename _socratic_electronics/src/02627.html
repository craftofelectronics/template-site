Answer the following questions about this amplifier test:

1.  Ideally, what should the output spectrum look like?

2.  What name do we give to the additional peaks seen in the output signal spectrum analysis?

3.  What does the presence of these peaks indicate about the amplifier circuit?

4.  Identify some electrical causes (within a typical amplifier circuit) that could account for these additional peaks.

1.  Ideally, what should the output spectrum look like? The output spectrum should be identical to the input spectrum, ideally.

2.  What name do we give to the additional peaks seen in the output signal spectrum analysis? These are called harmonics.

3.  What does the presence of these peaks indicate about the amplifier circuit? The amplifier is distorting the signal to some extent.

4.  Identify some electrical causes (within a typical amplifier circuit) that could account for these additional peaks. Improper biasing, poor circuit design (non-ideal component values), excessive loading.
