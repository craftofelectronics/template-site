Calculate the proper value of resistance R2 needs to be in order to draw 40% of the total current in this circuit:


$\epsfbox{01749x01.eps}$

R2 = 1.5 kΩ 

Follow-up question: explain how you could arrive at a rough estimate of R2’s necessary value without doing any algebra. In other words, show how you could at least set limits on R2’s value (i.e. "We know it has to be less than . . ." or "We know it has to be greater than . . .").

This is an interesting problem to solve algebraically from the current divider formula. I recommend using the product-over-sum formula for parallel resistance if you plan on doing this algebraically. The estimation question (in the follow-up) is also very good to discuss with your students. It is possible to at least "bracket" the value of R2 between two different resistance values without doing any math more complex than simple (fractional) arithmetic.

Of course, a less refined approach to solving this problem would be to assume a certain battery voltage and work with numerical figures – but what fun is that?
