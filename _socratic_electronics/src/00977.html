Knowing that the voltage between points A and B is zero, ask your students what the voltage drops must be across the biasing resistors. This question foreshadows the concept of a "virtual ground" in operational amplifier circuits. It is the idea that two or more points in a circuit may be held at the same potential without actually being connected together. In other words, the points are virtually common rather than being actually common with one another.
