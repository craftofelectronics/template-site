Impedance is defined as the complex ratio of voltage and current:


${\bf Z} = {{\bf V}\over {\bf I}}$

We may determine the complex definition of impedance provided by an capacitor if we consider the equation relating capacitor voltage and capacitor current:


$i = C {d \over dt} [ v(t) ]$

First, substitute a phasor expression of voltage into the above equation, then differentiate with respect to time to obtain an expression for current:


v(t) = ejωt


$i = C {d \over dt} \left[ e^{j \omega t} \right]$

Then, divide the two phasor expressions to obtain an expression for capacitive impedance.


${\bf Z_C} = {1 \over {j \omega C}} \hbox{\hskip 30pt or \hskip 30pt} {\bf Z_C} = -j {1 \over {\omega C}}$

Follow-up question: explain why the following expression for capacitive impedance is equivalent to the one shown above:


${\bf Z_C} = {1 \over {\omega C}} \> e^{j (-\pi / 2)}$

In order to solve this problem, your students must remember the basic rule for differentiating exponential functions:


${d \over dx} \left[ e^{ax} \right] = a e^{ax}$

This is one of the beauties of representing sinusoidal voltages and currents in complex exponential (phasor) form: it makes differentiation and integration relatively easy! In this sense, the Euler relation of ejx = cosx + jsinx is a transform function, transforming one type of mathematical problem into a different (easier) type.
