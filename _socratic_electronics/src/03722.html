Transistors act as controlled current sources. That is, with a fixed control signal in, they tend to regulate the amount of current going through them. Design an experimental circuit to prove this tendency of transistors. In other words, how could you demonstrate this current-regulating behavior to be a fact?


$\epsfbox{03722x01.eps}$

Procedure: measure the voltage dropped across RC while varying VCC, for several different values of IB (inferred by measuring voltage drop across RB).

Here, students must think like an experimental scientist: figuring out how to prove the relative stability of one variable despite variations in another, while holding the controlling variable constant. Encourage your students to actually build this circuit!
