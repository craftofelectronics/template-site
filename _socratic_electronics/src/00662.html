All electrical sources contain some internal impedance. This explains why voltage sources "sag" when placed under load:


$\epsfbox{00662x01.eps}$

In this diagram, the source’s internal impedance has been "lumped" into a single component, labeled ZTh, the Thévenin impedance. This intrinsic impedance naturally limits the amount of power any source can deliver to a load. It also creates a condition where load power is optimized at a particular load impedance.
