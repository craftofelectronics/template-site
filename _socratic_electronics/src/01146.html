Define the following terms, as they relate to phase-locked loop circuits:

 •  Center frequency

 •  Lock range

 •  Capture range

 •  Lock-up time

 •  Center frequency: the "free-running" frequency of a PLL’s voltage-controlled oscillator.

 •  Lock range: the range of input signal frequencies that a PLL is able to remain "locked in" to.

 •  Capture range: the range of input signal frequencies that a PLL is able to lock in to, from an unlocked state.

 •  Lock-up time: the amount of time required for a PLL to "lock in" to a given input frequency, from a cold start.

Ask your students to explain which range – lock or capture – is narrower, and why.
