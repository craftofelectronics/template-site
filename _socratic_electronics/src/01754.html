Use circuit simulation software to verify your predicted and measured parameter values.

Be sure to remind your students that resistances R1 and R2 may need to be series-parallel networks in themselves, to achieve the necessary values. An alternative you may wish to permit is the use of 10-turn (precision) potentiometers connected as rheostats for R1 and R2. This way the circuit’s minimum and maximum values may be precisely calibrated. The main potentiometer, Rpot1, should be a 3/4 turn unit, to allow fast checking of minimum and maximum total resistance, and it should be some common value such as 1 kΩ  or 10 kΩ .
