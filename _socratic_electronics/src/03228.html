Follow-up question: is this circuit truly equivalent to the original shown in the question? Sure, it responds the same under extreme conditions (open-circuit and short-circuit), but will it respond the same as the original circuit under modest load conditions (say, with a 5 kΩ  resistor connected across the load terminals)?

The purpose of this question is to get students thinking about Thévenin equivalent circuits from the perspective of how the original circuit responds to extreme variations in load resistance.

This question is also a good review of voltmeter and ammeter behavior: that ideal voltmeters act as open circuits (infinite input resistance) while ideal ammeters act as short circuits (zero input impedance).
