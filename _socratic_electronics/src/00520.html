Follow-up question #1: how must the battery and generator voltages compare in order for the battery to have current forced "backward" through it?

Follow-up question #2: which is the source component, and which is the load component in a circuit where a generator charges a secondary-cell battery? Explain your reasoning.

It is very important for students to understand the direction of current through a battery, both when charging and when discharging. A helpful perspective on this topic is to think of the battery either as being a source of electrical energy versus being a load. Under which conditions (charging or discharging) is the battery playing the role of source versus load?
