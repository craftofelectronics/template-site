Suppose you needed to choose a fixed resistor value (R) to make a voltage divider circuit, given a known potentiometer resistance value, the source voltage value, and the desired range of adjustment:


$\epsfbox{03102x01.eps}$

Solve for R, and show the equation you set up in order to do it.

Hint: remember the series resistor voltage divider formula . . .


$V_R = V_{total}\left( {R \over R_{total}} \right)$

R =  20.588 kΩ 

Be sure to have your students set up their equations in front of the class so everyone can see how they did it. Some students may opt to apply Ohm’s Law to the solution of R, which is good, but for the purpose of developing equations to fit problems it might not be the best solution. Challenge your students to come up with a single equation that solves for R, with all known quantities on the other side of the "equal" sign.
