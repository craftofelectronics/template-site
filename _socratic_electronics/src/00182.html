Suppose an electric current of 1.5 microamps (1.5 μA) were to go through a resistance of 2.3 mega-ohms (2.3 MΩ ). How much voltage would be "dropped" across this resistance? Show your work in calculating the answer.

1. 5 × 10 − 6 amps of current through a resistance of 2. 3 × 106 Ω  will produce a voltage "drop" equal to 3.45 volts.

It is important for students to understand that metric prefixes are nothing more than "shorthand" forms of scientific notation, with each prefix corresponding to a specific power-of-ten.
