When an electric current travels through an electrical resistance, not only will there be a voltage "drop" across that resistance (V = IR), but there will also be energy dissipated by that resistance. We describe the rate of energy dissipation over time as power (P), and we express power in the unit of the watt (W).

Write the equation relating power to voltage and current, for an electrical resistance. Also, describe what physical form this dissipated energy usually takes.


P = IV

Energy dissipated by an electrical resistance is usually manifest as heat, although sometimes it is also in the form of light.
