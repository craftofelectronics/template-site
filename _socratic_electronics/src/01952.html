Use circuit simulation software to verify your predicted and measured parameter values.

I have had great success with the following values:

 •  VCC = 7 to 24 volts

 •  C1 and C2 = 0.22 μF

 •  C3 = 0.47 μF

 •  L1 = 100 μH (ferrite core RF choke)

 •  R1 = 22 kΩ 

 •  R2 = 1.5 MΩ 

 •  Q1 = part number 2N3403

With these component values, the output waveform was quite clean and the frequency was very close to predicted:


$f_{out} = {1 \over {2 \pi \sqrt{L C_1 C_2 \over C_1 + C_2}}}$

You might want to quiz your students on the purpose of resistor R2, since it usually only has to be present at power-up to initiate oscillation!

An extension of this exercise is to incorporate troubleshooting questions. Whether using this exercise as a performance assessment or simply as a concept-building lab, you might want to follow up your students’ results by asking them to predict the consequences of certain circuit faults.
