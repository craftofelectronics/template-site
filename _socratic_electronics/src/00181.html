What units of measurement are used to express quantities of voltage, current, and resistance?

 •  Voltage is measured in the unit of the volt (V).

 •  Current is measured in the unit of the ampere, or amp (A).

 •  Resistance is measured in the unit of the ohm (Ω ).

Like all other physical quantities, there are units of measurement appropriate for specifying electrical quantities. In this sense, the "volt," "amp," or "ohm" are no different from "gram," "yard," or "bushel."
