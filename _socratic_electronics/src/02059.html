The "transconductance" ratio (gm) of a field-effect transistor is a very important device parameter. In essence, it describes the amplifying power of the transistor. Give a mathematical definition for this parameter, and provide some typical values from transistor datasheets.

Transconductance is defined as the ratio between drain current and gate voltage. I’ll let you research some typical values. Here are some transistor part numbers you could research datasheets for:

Challenge question: the "beta" ratio for a BJT (β) may be defined as the direct ratio of collector current to base current, or as the ratio between change in collector current and change in base current, as the following equations show.


$\beta_{DC} = {I_C \over I_B} \hbox{\hskip 20pt (DC current gain for a BJT)}$


$\beta_{AC} = {\Delta I_C \over \Delta I_B} \hbox{\hskip 20pt (AC current gain for a BJT)}$

By contrast, the transconductance of a FET is always defined in terms of change, and never in terms of a direct ratio:


$g_m = {\Delta I_D \over \Delta V_G}$


$g_m \neq {I_D \over V_G}$

Explain why this is.
