What is common-mode voltage, and how should a differential amplifier (ideally) respond to it?

"Common-mode voltage" is the amount of voltage common to both inputs of a differential amplifier. Ideally, a differential amplifier should reject this common-mode voltage, only amplifying the difference between the two input voltages.
