Explain why this description of a comparator is appropriate. What exactly is meant by the term "analog-to-digital converter," or ADC?

All ADCs input one or more analog signals and output a discrete signal.

This description of a comparator is not just theoretical. In many practical ADC circuits, a comparator is actually used as the primary analog-to-digital conversion device. This is particularly true for oversampling or Sigma-Delta converters, which may be built around a single (1-bit) comparator.
