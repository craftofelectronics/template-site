The formula for calculating the reluctance (ℜ) of an air-core wire coil ("solenoid") is as follows:


$\Re = {l \over {\mu_0 A}}$

Where,

l = linear length of coil in meters (m)

A = cross-sectional area of coil "throat" in square meters (m$^{2}$)

μ0 = permeability of free space = 4π  ×  10 − 7 (T ⋅ m/A)

Using this formula and the Rowland’s Law formula, calculate the amount of magnetic flux (Φ ) produced in the throat of an air-core solenoid with 250 turns of wire, a length of 0.2 meters, a cross-sectional area of 6.5  ×  10 − 4 square meters, and a coil current of 5 amps:
