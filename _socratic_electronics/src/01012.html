Practical integrator circuits must have a compensating resistor connected in parallel with the capacitor, in the feedback loop. Typically, this resistor value is very large: about 100 times as large as Rin.

This compensation resistor helps offset errors otherwise incurred by the opamp’s bias current on the inverting input.
