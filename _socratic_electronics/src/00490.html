Follow-up question: note the relationship between direction of current and polarity of voltage for each of the transformer windings. What do these different relationships suggest, in regard to the "flow" of power in the circuit?

One perspective that may help students understand the directions of current through each winding of the transformer, in relation to the voltage polarities, is to think of each winding as either being a source of electrical power or a load. Ask your students, "which winding acts as a source in this circuit, and which one acts as a load? Imagine these sources and loads are DC (so we may maintain the same polarity of voltage, for the sake of analysis). Which way would you draw the currents for a DC source and for a DC load?
