Follow-up question: note that the slope of both plots are approximately equal toward the far right end of the graph. Explain this effect in terms of magnetic saturation.

The purpose of this question is twofold: to get students to see that a ferromagnetic material such as iron is much more permeable (less "reluctant") than air, but that the great gains in B realized by iron tend to disappear as soon as saturation sets in. Once the iron is saturated, the gains in B for equal advances in H are the same as for air. That is, the $dB \over dH$ for iron is equal to the $dB \over dH $ for air once the iron is saturated.
