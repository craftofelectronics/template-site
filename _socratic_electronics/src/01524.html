Explain why the voltage gain (AV) of such an amplifier is approximately ${R_C \over R_E}$, using any or all of these general "rules" of transistor behavior:

 •  IE = IC + IB

 •  IE ≈ IC

 •  VBE ≈ 0. 7 volts

 •  $\beta = {I_C \over I_B}$

Remember that (AC) voltage gain is defined as ${\Delta V_{out} \over \Delta V_{in}}$. Hint: this question might be easier to answer if you first consider how to explain the unity-gain of a common-collector amplifier circuit (simply eliminate RC, replacing it with a direct connection to  − V, and consider VE to be the output voltage).

Since VBE is relatively constant, Δ Vin ≈ Δ VE. The next essential step in the explanation for the voltage gain formula is to couple this fact with IE ≈ IC. The rest I’ll leave for you to explain.
