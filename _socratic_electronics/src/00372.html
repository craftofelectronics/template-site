A 5 H inductor is subjected to an electric current that changes at a rate of 4.5 amps per second. How much voltage will be dropped by the inductor?

This inductor will drop 22.5 volts.

Don’t give your students the equation with which to perform this calculation! Let them find it on their own. The ${di \over dt}$ notation may be foreign to students lacking a strong mathematical background, but don’t let this be an obstacle to learning! Rather, use this as a way to introduce those students to the concept of rates of change, and to the calculus concept of the derivative.
