Explain what effect a shorted bilateral switch would have on the output waveform. Be as specific as possible.

If one of the bilateral switches were to fail shorted, it would skew all the arbitrary waveform voltages "toward" the one with the failed switch, distorting the waveform from its original shape.

A failed-shorted bilateral switch would create a sort of passive averager circuit between the shorted-switch pot and the selected-switch pot. If your students have difficulty figuring out the effects of this fault, remind them of what a passive averager is, and how it works.
