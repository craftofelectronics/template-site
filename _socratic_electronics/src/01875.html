If a coil of insulated wire is wrapped around an iron core, an inductance will be formed. Even if the wire has negligible resistance, the current through the coil from an AC source will be limited by the inductive reactance (XL) of the coil, as the magnetic flux in the iron core oscillates back and forth to induce a counter-EMF:

There is a simple formula (albeit containing a derivative term) describing the relationship between instantaneous flux (ϕ) and instantaneous induced voltage (v). Your students ought to know what it is, and that it should be applied to this question!
