Suppose a GFCI is rated to "trip" with a ground fault current of 5 mA. Given a source voltage of 115 volts, calculate the ground fault resistance range required to trip.

Any resistance value between 0 Ω  to 23 kΩ 

This question requires the use of Ohm’s Law, but to calculate a range of resistance values and not just a singular value. All students should be able to obtain the figure of 23 kΩ , but some may be confused as to whether this is a minimum or a maximum resistance value needed to trip the GFCI. Discuss this with your students, asking them to explain how we know 23 kΩ  is a maximum figure and not a minimum figure.
