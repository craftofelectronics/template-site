Multi-stage amplifiers may be direct-coupled, capacitor-coupled, or transformer-coupled. Explain why different forms of stage-to-stage coupling matters. In other words, identify at least one reason why a designer would choose one form of coupling over the other in their amplifier circuit.

Only direct-coupled amplifiers can pass DC signals through from the input to the output. Capacitor- and transformer-coupled amplifiers are immune to DC bias shifts in the input signal.
