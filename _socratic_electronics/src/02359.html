The energy efficiency (η) of switching converter circuits typically remains fairly constant over a wide range of voltage conversion ratios. Describe how a switching regulator circuit (controlling load voltage to a pre-set value) "appears" to a power source of changing voltage if the regulator’s load is constant. In other words, as the input voltage changes, what does the input current do?

The input current of a switching regulator is inversely proportional to the input voltage when powering a constant load, appearing as a negative impedance to the power source.

"Negative impedance" and "negative resistance" are phrases that may not be addressed very often in a basic electronics curriculum, but they have important consequences. If students experience difficulty understanding what the meaning of "negative" impedance is, remind them of this mathematical definition for impedance:


$Z = {dV \over dI}$

One of the unintended (and counter-intuitive) consequences of a circuit element with negative impedance can be oscillation, especially when the input power circuit happens to contain substantial inductance.
