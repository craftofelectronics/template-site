Here, students must choose the right type of series RC circuit configuration to provide the requested phase shift. This, of course, also involves choosing proper values for C1 and R1, and being able to successfully measure phase shift with an oscilloscope.

I recommend selecting a phase shift angle (Θ ) somewhere between 15$^{o}$ and 75$^{o}$. Angles too close to 90$^{o}$ will result in small output voltages that are difficult to measure through the noise.

An extension of this exercise is to incorporate troubleshooting questions. Whether using this exercise as a performance assessment or simply as a concept-building lab, you might want to follow up your students’ results by asking them to predict the consequences of certain circuit faults.
