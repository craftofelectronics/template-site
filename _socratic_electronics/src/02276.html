At the heart of an FM transmitter is a circuit called a voltage-controlled oscillator, or VCO. Explain what the purpose of a VCO is, and how this directly relates to frequency modulation.

A VCO generates an AC output signal whose frequency is proportional to an externally-supplied input voltage.

Note to your students that VCO circuits are not just used in FM radio transmission. They are also essential to the function of phase-locked loops.
