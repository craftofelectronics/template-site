The saturation, active, and breakdown regions on this graph are equivalent to the same regions in bipolar junction transistor characteristic curves.

The drain current regulation point is established by the gate voltage while in the "active" region.

Ask your students what the characteristic curves would look like for a perfect transistor: one that was a perfect regulator of drain current over the full range of drain-source voltage.
